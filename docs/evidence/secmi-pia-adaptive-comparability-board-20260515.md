## Interpretation

SecMI is strong and real. The stat head beats PIA on the aligned same-sample
checks, and the NNS auxiliary head is much stronger on the full split.

That does not create a new admitted consumer row. The aligned stat checks are
highly rank-correlated with PIA (`~0.907` Spearman) and disagree on only about
`12%` of binary decisions. This supports SecMI as corroborating Research
evidence, not as a separate product-facing signal with independent consumer
semantics.

The admission blocker is also still concrete. PIA rows carry structured
`adaptive_check` and cost fields. SecMI stat / NNS rows do not have a bounded
repeated-query adaptive review or an admitted-row cost schema. NNS has the
additional problem that it is an auxiliary scorer with no product-facing scorer
contract.

## Verdict

`keep_secmi_as_supporting_reference`.

No Platform or Runtime admitted bundle change is recommended. No SecMI GPU
release is recommended. Do not run a larger SecMI packet, promote NNS, build a
learned fusion/gating matrix, or present SecMI as an admitted PIA replacement.

The next valid Research action remains Lane A asset acquisition unless a new
SecMI consumer schema and adaptive protocol are explicitly opened first.

Based on your prompt and notes, the real target is not “a better recon,” but a genuinely different black-box signal family on the admitted DDPM/CIFAR10, final-output/query-only surface, with bounded cost and four-metric reporting. Your own materials also make recon the current decision-grade mainline, CLiD a strong but effectively gray-box corroborator, and variation the only clearly weaker-access black-box challenger lineage so far.      

After checking the 2024–2025 literature, my main conclusion is this: I do **not** see a fully mature fourth single-image black-box diffusion family that is already decision-grade and clearly outside reconstruction/variation behavior. The strongest diffusion-specific black-box results still cluster around image-to-image probing: REDIFFUSE/ICML 2025 uses repeated variation and average-back comparison; *GenAI Confessions* uses strength sweeps plus a logistic regression over seed–output distance vectors; CLiD/NeurIPS 2024 is powerful but depends on internal noise-prediction access; and CDI/CVPR 2025 explicitly moves to **set-level statistical aggregation** because single-image MIAs are often too weak for large diffusion models. So the best “new” black-box path is probably not a wholly different primitive, but a new way of turning weak probe responses into stable evidence: response distributions, spectral/structural slicing, counterfactual probes, and statistical aggregation. ([Proceedings of Machine Learning Research][1])

For the cost estimates below, I’m assuming a **512-sample screening split** (256 members + 256 non-members), the repo’s DDPM/CIFAR10 screen surface, and roughly **2 seconds/query on one GPU**; full 1000+1000 confirmation runs scale roughly linearly from there.    

## Hypothesis 1: Response-Cloud Geometry

**Signal/Method:** For one query image, run 8–12 image-to-image/denoise probes at one or two fixed strengths or timesteps. Do **not** score original-vs-reconstruction distance. Instead, score the **geometry of the output cloud itself**: mean pairwise LPIPS/SSIM, covariance trace, top-eigenvalue ratio, feature-space entropy, and optional KDE/LLR against shadow-fitted (Q_{\text{in}}) and (Q_{\text{out}}).

**Intuition:** A member should sit in a tighter local attraction basin, so repeated probes produce a lower-variance, lower-intrinsic-dimension cloud. REDIFFUSE and *GenAI Confessions* already show that in-training images produce less variation under image-to-image probing, while black-box generative-model embedding work and GLiRA suggest that response sets can be statistically embedded and calibrated rather than collapsed to one scalar. ([Proceedings of Machine Learning Research][1])

**Literature support:** REDIFFUSE (ICML 2025), *GenAI Confessions* (2025 arXiv), GLiRA (2024 arXiv), and data-kernel/statistical-inference work on black-box generative models (2024–2025). ([Proceedings of Machine Learning Research][1])

**Execution plan:** On DDPM/CIFAR10, use two timesteps such as (t \in {150,250}), 4–6 probes each. Embed outputs with a CPU-cheap feature stack first (SSIM, DCT energy bands, shallow perceptual features). Train only a tiny logistic regressor or KDE on a public control split.

**Cost:** 8–12 queries/sample, about **2.3–3.4 GPU-hours** for the 512-sample screen; CPU post-processing is minor. No full shadow model required.

**Expected performance:** My prior is **AUC 0.78–0.88** and **TPR@1%FPR 0.30–0.55** on the screen. I would treat anything above that as a strong signal worth promoting.

**Distinctness:** This is not recon, because the score is **output–output geometry**, not original–output similarity. It is also not semantic classification, because the signal never asks “what is in the image.”

## Hypothesis 2: Strength-Response Curve

**Signal/Method:** Probe the same image at 4–5 strengths or timesteps and model the **shape of the retention curve**: slope, curvature, area under the curve, inflection point, and heteroscedasticity across strengths. Use the curve shape itself as the signal, not a single reconstruction score.

**Intuition:** Members should degrade more slowly as seed influence is relaxed; the membership signal may live in the **phase transition** from “identity-preserving” to “identity-forgetting.” *GenAI Confessions* shows that separation grows with strength, while SeqMIA argues more generally that the **pattern of a metric sequence** can be more informative than any isolated metric. Your own temporal-signal note points in the same direction. ([arXiv][2]) 

**Literature support:** *GenAI Confessions* (strength sweep), SeqMIA (2024), and the repo’s temporal-signal notes. ([arXiv][2]) 

**Execution plan:** Use strengths like (s={0.15,0.35,0.55,0.75}) or equivalent timesteps, with 2 repeats each. Fit a tiny curve-feature classifier. A CPU-first version can use only SSIM/DCT retention.

**Cost:** 8–10 queries/sample, about **2.3–2.8 GPU-hours** for the 512-sample screen.

**Expected performance:** My prior is **AUC 0.80–0.89** and **TPR@1%FPR 0.35–0.60**.

**Distinctness:** This is the **least novel** of the five because it is adjacent to variation-style attacks, but it is still a different family from plain REDIFFUSE-style averaging because the signal is the **curve shape**, not a single averaged distance.

## Hypothesis 3: Frequency-Sliced Stability Probe

**Signal/Method:** Create low-pass, band-pass, and high-frequency-attenuated versions of the same query image before probing. Then compare how stable the outputs remain across these spectral slices. Score the **difference of differences** across bands.

**Intuition:** The 2025 frequency-domain paper argues that existing diffusion MIAs are confounded by diffusion models’ weakness on high-frequency information; the 2024 structural memorization paper argues that members preserve **structure** better than non-members and are more robust to distortions. That suggests a black-box route: do not ask whether the whole image reconstructs well, ask **which spectral/structural content survives probing unusually well**. ([arXiv][3])

**Literature support:** Frequency-domain enhancement for diffusion MIA (2025) and structural memorization in text-to-image diffusion models (2024). ([arXiv][3])

**Execution plan:** Three input views × 3 probes each = 9 total queries. Compute spectral-retention features, pairwise cloud dispersion, and band-wise stability gaps. Start with FFT/DCT features only; add LPIPS later only if needed.

**Cost:** 9 queries/sample, about **2.6 GPU-hours** for the 512-sample screen; CPU transforms are trivial.

**Expected performance:** As a **standalone** family, I would target **AUC 0.72–0.84**. As a fusion feature added to H1/H2, I would expect a more realistic **+0.03 to +0.06 AUC** gain than a full replacement.

**Distinctness:** This is a true new signal family: **spectral sensitivity**, not reconstruction, not semantics. The risk is that the current literature mostly validates frequency as a plug-in enhancer, not yet as a fully black-box standalone attack.

## Hypothesis 4: Counterfactual Patch-Recovery Probe

**Signal/Method:** Apply structured occlusions or cutout masks to the query image, then probe the model from the corrupted seed. Score whether the model recovers the masked content or preserves global coherence **better than expected**.

**Intuition:** If the model memorized the sample, it may recover missing local content from a damaged seed more faithfully. Cross-domain black-box MIAs in LVLMs and multimodal RAG show that **counterfactual perturbations** can force a model to rely on memorized information rather than generic priors; structural memorization in diffusion suggests that member structure is also better preserved. ([arXiv][4])

**Literature support:** KCMP for LVLMs (2025), MrM for multimodal RAG (2025), and structural memorization in diffusion (2024). ([arXiv][4])

**Execution plan:** Use 4 mask patterns and 2 repeats each. For CIFAR10, center patch, random 8×8, random 16×16, and stripe masks are enough. Score masked-region SSIM/LPIPS and whole-image coherence drift.

**Cost:** 8 queries/sample, about **2.3 GPU-hours** for the 512-sample screen.

**Expected performance:** My prior is lower here: **AUC 0.68–0.82** and **TPR@1%FPR 0.20–0.45**. It is the most novel and the least directly validated on diffusion-image MIAs.

**Distinctness:** This is neither whole-image reconstruction nor semantic classification. It is a **local counterfactual-completion** family.

## Hypothesis 5: Micro-Bag Statistical Audit

**Signal/Method:** Convert one image into a small **bag of near-identity variants**—small crops, tiny color shifts, one spectral slice, one masked view—and run a very weak probe on each. Aggregate with a sign test, Welch test, or a tiny scorer over the bag-level feature vector.

**Intuition:** Single-image black-box signals are often sparse. CDI shows that selective aggregation plus rigorous testing can turn weak MIA signals into strong evidence at the dataset level, and SeqMIA plus strength-vector methods suggest that aggregating many weak configurations is often better than trying to find one perfect scalar. This is the most practical route for low-FPR honesty. ([CVF开放获取][5])

**Literature support:** CDI (CVPR 2025), SeqMIA (2024), and *GenAI Confessions*’ vector-of-probes setup. ([CVF开放获取][5])

**Execution plan:** Start with 8 variants and 1 weak probe each; only escalate to 12–16 total queries when the bag-level test is near the decision boundary. For portfolio-style auditing, promote the same machinery directly to a CDI-style set test.

**Cost:** Adaptive **8→12 queries/sample**, about **2.3–3.4 GPU-hours** for the 512-sample screen.

**Expected performance:** For single-image bags, I would target **AUC 0.80–0.90**. For set-level auditing, the ceiling is likely higher and much more defensible at low FPR.

**Distinctness:** The power comes from **statistical aggregation over variants**, not from any single reconstruction or semantic classifier.

The cross-cutting wrapper I would add to all five is **adaptive query allocation**. MIDST 2025 shows that attack efficacy depends strongly on noise/time-step choice, and your own query-budget notes already point toward bandit-style or learned allocation. In practice, I would spend 4 “seed” probes first, then allocate the remaining 4–8 only when uncertainty is still high. ([arXiv][6]) 

My priority order would be **H1 → H3 → H5 → H2 → H4**. H1 is the best bet for a genuinely distinct sample-level family; H3 is the cheapest orthogonal enhancer; H5 is the best low-FPR honesty play. I would not spend the first cycle on full LiRA-style multi-shadow density estimation: your own notes already flag the shadow-model overhead, and in this setting it looks more like a calibration layer than a new signal family.

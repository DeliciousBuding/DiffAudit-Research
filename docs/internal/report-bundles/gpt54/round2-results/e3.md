On the admitted DDPM/CIFAR10 surface, GSA is already at AUC `0.998` / TPR@1%FPR `0.987`, while the weak loss-style line is around AUC `0.699463` / TPR@1%FPR `0.03125`; and your white-box threat model explicitly grants gradients, activations, parameters, and arbitrary forward/backward computation, so sampler-side wrappers do not count as closure.    

My read after checking the 2024–2025 primary papers is that the internal ranking basically survives contact with the literature: **H1 activation-subspace fingerprint** is the cleanest distinct second attack family; **H4 training-free risky-subspace pruning** is the best post-training defense; **H2 score-vector geometry** is the fastest practical sidecar; **H3 curvature/self-influence** is the strongest paper bet but still adjacent to GSA’s parameter-side story; and **H5 targeted unlearning / PEFT** is the heavier fallback, not the first bounded pilot. That ordering is exactly what the recent mechanism papers now support: informative neurons matter in white-box MIA; diffusion memorization can localize to single neurons or narrow shared subspaces; score/output geometry has become a real attack family via SimA; and second-order diffusion influence is real, but more mature as attribution than as a direct MIA recipe. ([arXiv][1])

## 1. Reordered priority

1. **H1 — Activation-subspace fingerprint attack.** Best **clean distinct second family**. It is internal-state based, not loss-based, not first-order-gradient based, and matches the strongest mechanism story from *Unveiling the Unseen*, *Finding NeMo*, and the shared-subspace deletion paper. ([arXiv][1])
2. **H4 — Training-free risky-subspace pruning defense.** Best **post-training defense**. It directly targets the “localized risky subspace” hypothesis and is the best way to move beyond the current single-selector falsifier. *Finding NeMo* and the shared-subspace paper are the memorization-specific anchors; *ConceptPrune* and *CURE* are not memorization papers, but they show that training-free targeted pruning/projection in diffusion models is feasible and robust. ([神经信息处理系统会议论文集][2])
3. **H2 — Score-vector geometry attack.** Best **fast sidecar**, but not the cleanest flagship white-box successor because it is also a natural gray-box family. SimA gives this line real standing, and the 2025 frequency paper makes it stronger as a plug-in. ([arXiv][3])
4. **H3 — Curvature-preconditioned self-influence.** Best **paper bet**, not first engineering bet. The theory from IHA is strong, and diffusion-specific influence/K-FAC is now real, but this is still parameter-side and therefore more GSA-adjacent than H1. ([arXiv][4])
5. **H5 — Targeted unlearning / PEFT regularization.** Real, promising, but heavier. SISS, MemControl, and the 2025 regularization paper all make it defensible, but it is a fine-tuning branch, not the first bounded scout. ([arXiv][5])

Public code also exists for NeMo, SimA, SISS, and diffusion-influence, so the top-ranked lines are implementation-legible rather than purely conceptual. ([GitHub][6])

## 2. Best attack family to implement first

**H1 is the right answer** if the question is “what is the next genuinely worth-doing distinct white-box family?” It is the only candidate that is both clearly non-loss / non-GSA and still genuinely white-box. The strongest 2024–2025 evidence says hidden neurons/activations alone can carry strong membership information, and diffusion memorization can be concentrated in very small neuron/subspace regions. That makes H1 the cleanest translation of current mechanism evidence into the admitted DDPM/CIFAR10 audit surface. ([arXiv][1])

One important repo-specific correction: do **not** port SD1.4 cross-attention selectors literally. Your own notes already say the active admitted surface is DDPM/CIFAR10, not the paper-faithful `SD1.4 / LAION / memorized prompts / SSCD` protocol, and the current NeMo-style DDPM packet is already a bounded falsifier rather than an active intake dossier. So the right move is to port the **mechanism**—localized internal leakage—not the original layer recipe. Hook DDPM U-Net scales, not text cross-attention values.  

If you optimize purely for **speed**, H2 may land first. If you optimize for the actual white-box question—**clean second family**—H1 is the better flagship. ([arXiv][3])

## 3. Best post-training defense to implement first

**H4 is the best post-training defense**. The current negative Finding-NeMo-style packet only falsifies one fixed-mask intervention; it does **not** falsify the broader claim that leakage sits in a shared risky subspace that can be edited out post hoc. The strongest defense story in the recent literature is exactly this: localize a shared risky region, then apply a one-time training-free edit or pruning step. That faces a white-box attacker directly in a way sampler-side tricks cannot.   ([神经信息处理系统会议论文集][2])

I would **not** start with AMG-style or other inference-time guidance methods here. They are meaningful memorization-mitigation papers, but in your threat model an attacker can simply access the model weights and internal computation directly, so a deployment-time wrapper is not a real closure.  ([arXiv][7])

H5 is the correct fallback if H4 is too weak: SISS is the first diffusion data-unlearning method with theoretical guarantees, MemControl shows tiny PEFT subsets can move the memorization/quality frontier, and the regularization paper gives the cleanest theory case for why this should work. But it is still a bounded fine-tuning program, so it should come **after** the training-free edit line. ([arXiv][5])

## 4. Minimal implementation package for each line

**H1 — activation/state family**
Hook **3 U-Net sites** at three spatial scales: one late down block, the mid block, and one early up block. Use **3 fixed timesteps** to start. Features: per-channel mean absolute activation, variance, sparsity, and 4–8 PCA coefficients from the channel vector. Rank channels on shadows by member-vs-control effect size, then fit **logistic regression first**; only escalate to a tiny MLP if LR is stable and obviously underfitting. Use **3 shadows × (64 members + 64 controls)** for selection/calibration, then a blind target `64/64` board. Cost target: about **0.5–1.5 GPU hours** plus CPU minutes.  

**H2 — score-output geometry family**
No hooks. Start with either **one SimA-style timestep** chosen on shadows, or a small **4-timestep grid** if the single point is unstable. Features: `||ε̂_t||_2`, one cross-timestep cosine, and one low-pass / frequency-filtered residual feature. Threshold first; LR only if the multi-feature vector is clearly better. Same `3-shadow + blind target 64/64` structure. Cost target: about **0.25–0.75 GPU hours**. ([arXiv][3]) 

**H3 — curvature / influence family**
Do **not** start with online K-FAC. Reuse existing GSA gradient dumps if you have them. Minimal feature: `gᵀ(F_diag+λI)^{-1}g` at **1–2 layers** and **2 timesteps**, with two dampings. Compare directly to raw gradient norms on the same extraction. Threshold or LR, shadow-calibrated. If you need live extraction, enforce the repo’s `<=4h` pilot budget. ([arXiv][4])  

**H4 — post-training subspace edit**
Use the same H1 layers and timesteps. Build a risky subspace from **member-vs-control mean/covariance contrast** on the shadow surface or owner-side target fit split. Then try edits in order: **channel zeroing**, **low-rank projection removing 1–4 risky PCs**, then **0.05%–0.5% structured pruning**. Re-attack the defended model with **GSA + H1 + H2**, not just one scorer. Cost target: roughly **0.5–1h** for identification; evaluation dominates. ([神经信息处理系统会议论文集][2]) 

**H5 — unlearning / PEFT family**
Pick the top **32–64 risky members** from `GSA ∪ H1`. Update only **late/mid U-Net blocks** or a small LoRA/PEFT mask with a short early-stopped run. Prefer a SISS-style unlearning objective or a MemControl-style PEFT subset search if you already have the scaffolding. Re-attack with GSA + H1 + H2. Cost target: **2–4 GPU hours**. ([arXiv][5]) 

## 5. 64-sample bounded scout

Use the repo’s existing discipline: `64/64` first, low-FPR first, then only promote if stable at `128/256/512`. For attacks, fit **only on shadows** and keep the target `64/64` blind. For H4 defense, fit the edit on one owner-side half-split and evaluate on a disjoint owner-side half-split before you ever look at the full target board. Primary metrics stay the repo’s four-metric standard, with **TPR@1%FPR first** and **TPR@0.1%FPR second** because the tail is the real value regime.   

A scout deserves scale-up if it shows all three things:

* clear tail separation above the weak loss line, not just prettier AUC;
* stability across shadow folds / nearby timesteps / nearby layers;
* and either nontrivial complementarity with GSA or a real drop in attack metrics after defense.

Because white-box baseline TPR@1%FPR is already `0.987`, the literal “beat baseline by ≥5%” rule cannot be the right scout gate for a second family; the honest gate here is **beats the weak auxiliary line + is nonredundant + survives larger packets**.   

## 6. Kill gates

**Kill H1** if the signal stays close to the loss line at low FPR, only appears at one fragile layer/timestep, or vanishes at `128/128`.
**Kill H2** if the best timestep shifts wildly across shadows, or the frequency add-on lifts AUC but not TPR@1%FPR.
**Kill H3** if diagonal-Fisher does not beat raw gradient norm on the same dumps, or if live K-FAC breaks the `<=4h` pilot budget. 
**Kill H4** if the edit only fools H1/H2 but leaves GSA unchanged, or if the gain appears only on fit IDs.
**Kill H5** if a short PEFT run already costs more than H4 and still cannot beat it on low-FPR reduction.

## 7. If the goal is to fill the white-box distinct second family fast

Do **H1 first**, with **H2 as the same-week sidecar**. That sequence is the fastest path to an honest second-family artifact:

1. hook 3 DDPM U-Net scales;
2. dump `64/64` activation summaries on shadows and target;
3. fit shadow-only LR on top activation features;
4. blind-evaluate target;
5. if tail signal is real, extend to `128/256`.

That is the best answer to your actual question, not just the cheapest thing to run.   ([arXiv][1])

## 8. If the goal is to get a more honest post-training defense than the current falsifier fast

Do **H1-derived H4**, not H5 and not sampler-side guidance. Concretely: identify risky directions from H1, try projection/zeroing/pruning in that order, then attack the defended model with **GSA + H1 + H2**. That gives you the best chance of producing a post-training defense that is both more honest than the current NeMo-style falsifier and actually relevant under the repo’s white-box threat model.   ([神经信息处理系统会议论文集][2])

Net: **H1 is the distinct second family. H4 is the first defense to run. H2 is the fast auxiliary line. H3 is the paper bet. H5 is the bounded fine-tuning fallback.**
